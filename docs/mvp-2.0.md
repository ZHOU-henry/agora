# MVP 2.0

## Goal

Upgrade Agora from a marketplace MVP into a first operating-system layer for
agent delivery:

- demand still starts the market
- response acceptance still creates the formal engagement
- but the platform now keeps delivery health, customer confirmation, field
  feedback, incident pressure, and expansion signals visible on the main role
  surfaces

## 2.0 Shape

- homepage becomes a platform control narrative instead of only a catalog-first
  landing page
- customer page becomes a deployment-operations workspace
- builder page becomes a delivery-and-expansion workspace
- ops page becomes a control tower for conversion, execution, review, and risk
- provider detail becomes a real builder operating profile, not just a supply
  card
- engagement detail becomes the canonical post-acceptance operating object

## Core Logic

1. demand is a market object
2. accepted response creates the delivery object
3. delivery object owns the run, deliverables, confirmation, feedback, and
   incidents
4. customer feedback stays attached to the same engagement
5. expansion should emerge from a healthy delivered engagement, not from losing
   context and starting over

## What 2.0 Adds

- shared engagement intelligence:
  - health posture
  - unresolved feedback count
  - open incident pressure
  - confirmation state
  - expansion signals
- role-first operating metrics across customer, builder, ops, and provider
  surfaces
- stronger product framing around context, execution, observability,
  collaboration, and governance

## Still Out Of Scope

- generalized quoting / procurement workflow
- enterprise identity and tenancy
- billing and settlement rails
- complex deployment orchestration beyond the current engagement model
